Hitesh Golchha

I am currently pursuing MS in CS from UMass Amherst. I have work and research experience in the fields of NLP, Conversational AI (language generation and understanding), Reinforcement Learning and Representation Learning from companies like Amazon, Flipkart and research labs at UMass Amherst, IIT Patna and Bar Ilan University.

I am currently pursuing research in the IESL Lab under Prof. Andrew McCallum , investigating ways to use LLMs and RL to play Text Based Games.

As an Applied Scientist intern at Amazon last summer, I worked on probing Product representations from vision, text, and multi-modal models for identifying products which have similar export-eligibility.

For two years at Flipkart (Walmart), I worked on problems in Conversational AI: intent detection, entity extraction, sentence segmentation and rescoring ASR beams. Challenges included handling multilinguality, class imbalance, miscalibration, augmentation and robustness. The voice assistants we developed are used by millions of Indians for E-commerce shopping every day. I also participated in hackathons on unsupervised reverse image search and question answering.

I did my B.Tech. in Computer Science and Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Patna, India. I was guided by Prof. Asif Ekbal and Prof. Pushpak Bhattacharyya for my B.Tech Thesis and during my Junior Research Fellowship. My research endeavours were on Style transfer for dialog response generation, Natural Language Understanding for agents, Suggestion Mining, and Question answering.

I also did a short research internship at Bar Ilan University under Prof. Ido Dagan after my Junior Year on Proposition Coreference Resolution for generating Natural Language Knowledge graphs.

Courteously Yours: Inducing courteous behavior in Customer Care responses using Reinforced Pointer Generator Network
